Funnel Accounts

A monitoring system flags an account that receives numerous cash deposits from different individuals across several distant cities, with the funds quickly withdrawn or wired out from a single branch far from where the deposits were made. This pattern is best described as:

Examiner's Rationale

A — Correct. A funnel account collects many small, geographically dispersed cash deposits — often street-level proceeds — into one account, then rapidly moves the pooled funds out from a different location. FinCEN advisories link this pattern strongly to drug trafficking and other cash-intensive crime.

B — Incorrect. Nested correspondent banking involves one financial institution accessing correspondent services through another's account — a bank-to-bank relationship, not individual cash deposits.

C — Incorrect. Trade-based fraud relies on manipulated invoices and goods movements, not cash deposits and withdrawals.

D — Incorrect. A nominee director arrangement conceals corporate control — unrelated to the deposit-and-withdrawal geography described here.
